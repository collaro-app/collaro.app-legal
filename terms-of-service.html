8. Bookings and the Booking Process

An Owner initiates a booking by selecting a Provider, a service, dates and one or more dogs, and sending a request. A booking is not confirmed until the Provider accepts it; a Provider may accept or reject any request at their discretion, subject to availability and capacity limits enforced by the Platform.

When the Owner sends the request, the simulated payment is authorised (a hold is placed on the selected card); the request must still be accepted by the Provider before the booking is confirmed. Once a booking is accepted, the Owner and Provider can coordinate through in-app messaging. A booking is automatically marked completed by the Platform after the service period ends; there is no manual “mark complete” step and no built-in rescheduling.

A pending request that a Provider does not answer is automatically expired once its service date passes. In addition, if a Provider edits a service in a way that affects a still-pending request (including a change to its price), that pending request may be automatically cancelled, because the price quoted to the Owner is treated as binding for that request. Bookings that have already been accepted are not changed or re-priced by later edits.

You are responsible for reviewing the details of a booking before sending or accepting it. The arrangement, performance and completion of the service are the responsibility of the Owner and Provider involved.

9. Payments, Authorisation and Capture

Payments on the Platform are currently simulated. No real money changes hands, no real payment instrument is charged, and the authorisation, capture, void and refund steps are simulated by the Platform rather than processed by a bank or card network. Amounts are shown in New Israeli Shekels (₪).

In the simulated model: when the Owner sends a booking request, the Owner’s selected card is “authorised” (a hold), before the Provider has accepted; the payment is automatically “captured” a set period (currently about 48 hours) after the service ends, unless a dispute has been opened. Capture is automatic, but a refund is never automatic and is not something an Owner can trigger directly: a refund happens only when an administrator decides one while resolving a dispute, and the administrator chooses whether to grant a full or partial refund (see “Disputes”).

When you enter card details, the app transmits them to our servers to simulate the authorisation; we do not store your full card number, security code or cardholder name (see the Privacy Policy). There is currently no platform service fee, deposit, instalment or no-show charge.

If and when real payment processing is introduced, additional terms — including any service fees and the use of third-party payment processors — will be presented to you before they take effect.
